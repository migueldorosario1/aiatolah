3 Top Bargain Artificial Intelligence (AI) Stocks to Buy Right Now

The artificial intelligence revolution is reshaping the global economy, and no company embodies this transformation more vividly than Nvidia. Its data center GPUs have become the essential picks-and-shovels of the AI gold rush, driving triple-digit revenue growth and a soaring stock price. However, that success has also inflated Nvidia's valuation to levels that give many value-conscious investors pause. For those convinced of AI's long-term trajectory but unwilling to chase Nvidia at a premium, there are other high-quality companies deeply embedded in the AI infrastructure story that trade at more reasonable multiples. Here are three top bargain AI stocks to buy right now.

Advanced Micro Devices is the most direct rival to Nvidia in the high-performance compute market. While Nvidia holds a commanding lead in AI training, AMD's Instinct MI300 series accelerators are gaining traction for inference workloads and offer a competitive price-to-performance ratio. The company is also benefiting from a strong server CPU cycle with its EPYC processors, which frequently pair with AI accelerators in data center builds. Trading at a forward earnings multiple significantly below Nvidia's, AMD provides diversified exposure to AI, cloud computing, and PC refresh cycles without the same concentration risk. As enterprises optimize cost per query in AI deployments, AMD's hardware is well positioned to capture second-source and inference budgets.

Marvell Technology may not be a household name, but its custom application-specific integrated circuits (ASICs) and data infrastructure silicon are critical to hyperscale AI data centers. The company designs specialized chips for large cloud operators looking to optimize AI workloads beyond off-the-shelf GPUs. Marvell's electro-optics and networking products also address the bandwidth bottlenecks that arise when connecting thousands of accelerated computing nodes. With a growing pipeline of custom AI chip programs and a recovery in its non-data-center segments, Marvell's shares offer a bargain entry point relative to the explosive revenue growth projected over the next several years. The stock's recent pullback, tied partly to cyclical inventory corrections, masks the structural shift toward custom silicon that will define the next phase of AI infrastructure.

Qualcomm is often overlooked in the AI frenzy because it does not sell data center GPUs, but it is quietly positioning itself as the leader in on-device AI. Its Snapdragon X Elite and upcoming mobile platforms feature neural processing units capable of running large language models locally, reducing latency and cloud dependency. The shift toward AI-powered PCs and premium smartphones running generative AI applications will drive a significant upgrade cycle, and Qualcomm's licensing and chipset model captures value from nearly every device sold. Trading at a valuation that still reflects a mature mobile market, Qualcomm's AI potential is underappreciated, making it a compelling bargain for investors willing to look beyond the data center.

Of course, calling any stock a bargain in this overheated market requires caution. Each of these companies carries execution risk, and none will replicate Nvidia's current dominance in AI training. However, their collective exposure to the AI buildout—through accelerators, custom silicon, and edge inference—offers a more balanced risk-reward profile. With valuations that leave room for positive surprises, AMD, Marvell, and Qualcomm deserve a place in a diversified AI portfolio.
