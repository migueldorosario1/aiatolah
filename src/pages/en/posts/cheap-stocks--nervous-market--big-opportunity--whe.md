Cheap Stocks, Nervous Market, Big Opportunity: Where to Put $10,000 Right Now

The stock market is trembling. Inflation data, trade policy whiplash, and geopolitical anxiety have shaken indices and sent the VIX climbing. Yet for the disciplined investor, fear creates the very mispricing that builds generational wealth. At the center of this turbulence sits a name that has become synonymous with the artificial intelligence megatrend: Nvidia. After a sharp pullback, the stock now trades at a valuation that almost seems to ignore the company's dominance in AI compute, its accelerating data center revenue, and a product cycle that will be defined by the Blackwell platform. For anyone asking where to deploy $10,000 in a nervous market, the answer is not merely to buy Nvidia—it is to understand why this specific moment represents a rare alignment of a wide moat, a cheap relative price, and a structural growth story that will not be derailed by a tariff headline.

Nvidia shares have shed roughly a quarter of their value from recent highs. The selloff has been indiscriminate, driven by fears of export controls to China, a broader rotation out of momentum names, and some profit-taking after years of extraordinary gains. Yet the fundamentals tell a very different tale. The company just delivered another quarter of triple-digit growth in data center revenue, with hyperscalers and enterprises racing to build out the infrastructure for generative AI. The upcoming Blackwell architecture, which moves from chip-level to system-level optimization, will deliver a performance leap that entrenches Nvidia’s position as the picks-and-shovels provider for the AI era. Gross margins remain stratospheric because the competitive gap is so wide. AMD and the custom ASIC brigade are making inroads, but they are years behind on the software ecosystem that makes CUDA the default platform for developers. That ecosystem is not a feature; it is the moat.

What makes Nvidia look cheap today is not backward-looking multiples, but forward earnings power that the consensus repeatedly underestimates. The stock trades at around 25 times forward earnings estimates, a multiple that does not reflect a company growing revenue at 50% or more, with an expanding total addressable market that now extends beyond cloud to sovereign AI, automotive, robotics, and healthcare. Every efficiency gain in AI, every new model architecture, every startup that builds on Nvidia GPUs deepens the dependence on its stack. Even DeepSeek’s recent efficiency breakthrough, which briefly spooked the market into thinking it would collapse demand for compute, is likely to have the opposite effect: cheaper inference encourages more widespread deployment, and that ultimately requires more Nvidia hardware. Jevons paradox is alive and well in the AI industry.

Putting $10,000 to work right now demands a strategy that acknowledges both the opportunity and the near-term volatility. A prudent approach would allocate a core position—say, 70%—directly to Nvidia shares, leaving the remainder in cash or short-term Treasuries to buy any further dips. The idea is not to catch the absolute bottom, which is impossible, but to build a position while the market sentiment is still fragile and before the next earnings cycle resets the narrative. For those uncomfortable with single-stock risk, pairing Nvidia with a smaller stake in TSMC—the manufacturing backbone that makes the chips—creates a concentrated but logically diversified bet on the AI semiconductor supply chain. Both companies trade at valuations that look far more reasonable than they did six months ago, and both will benefit from the same secular spending wave.

The nervous market is offering a gift: a generational compounder at a price that assumes the AI story is stalling, when every leading indicator says it is just beginning. The infrastructure buildout alone will require hundreds of billions of dollars in investment over the next few years, and the return on that investment will accrue disproportionately to the company that owns the platform layer. Nvidia is not a trade. It is an asset to hold through the noise, and right now it is on sale.
