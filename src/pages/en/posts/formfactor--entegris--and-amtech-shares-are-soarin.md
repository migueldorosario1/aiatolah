FormFactor, Entegris, and Amtech are surging in today’s session, and the common thread runs straight through Nvidia’s sprawling AI infrastructure buildout. These three names represent critical but often overlooked links in the advanced packaging and process-control chain that Nvidia’s Blackwell and Hopper GPU ramps are straining to capacity.

FormFactor is benefiting directly from the explosion in high-bandwidth memory and advanced probe-card complexity. As Nvidia pushes CoWoS-based designs to the limit, chipmakers require far more exhaustive testing at the wafer level — a trend that lifts FormFactor’s orders and average selling prices. The market is repricing the stock not just on this cycle’s strength but on structural demand that will persist through 2026.

Entegris, the contamination-control and specialty-materials provider, is seeing a similar lift. Nvidia’s hyperscale customers are intolerant of defects in large compute tiles, and every node migration increases the consumption of ultrapure chemicals, filtration, and gas delivery systems. Entegris is leveraged to AI spend with high margin visibility, and the Street is starting to treat it as a secular AI play rather than a traditional semi-materials name.

Amtech, smaller and more volatile, is rallying as investors connect its thermal processing and wafer-handling tools to silicon carbide and power-device supply chains that are expanding to meet AI data-center energy requirements. While the Nvidia link here is indirect, the broader electrification of AI infrastructure validates Amtech’s positioning. Sentiment is bolstered by the view that all adjacent fab-equipment names will benefit from a sustained capex cycle.

Taken together, these moves underscore a real-time broadening of the AI trade. Nvidia remains the gravitational center, but the spending wave is now lifting a second tier of precision suppliers that are essential to keeping yields high and output scalable. The market is pricing in a multiyear investment cycle, not a one-off replacement wave.
