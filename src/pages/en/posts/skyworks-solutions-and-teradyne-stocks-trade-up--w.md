Nvidia's Gravity Pulls Skyworks and Teradyne Higher, Signaling a Broader AI Rebound

The upward moves in Skyworks Solutions and Teradyne shares this week are not random noise. They represent a downstream ripple from Nvidia's relentless expansion and the broader artificial intelligence infrastructure buildout. While Nvidia dominates the headlines for its data center GPUs, the ecosystem feeding that dominance is now catching a strong bid, and both Skyworks and Teradyne sit squarely in that slipstream.

Skyworks Solutions, a key supplier of radio-frequency components, is seeing renewed interest as AI-driven edge devices and data center networking demand higher-performance connectivity chips. Nvidia's next-generation systems require increasingly sophisticated wireless and wired interconnects, and Skyworks' technology is embedded in the infrastructure that ties AI clusters together. The trading up reflects a market realization that the AI hardware story extends well beyond the GPU itself, and Skyworks is a quiet beneficiary. Even without a direct Nvidia design win on every platform, the rising tide of AI-centric networking equipment lifts its addressable market materially.

Teradyne's move is equally logical when viewed through the Nvidia lens. The company's semiconductor test equipment is critical for advanced packaging and high-bandwidth memory (HBM) used in Nvidia's Blackwell and future architectures. As Nvidia scales its supply chain and pushes partners like TSMC for more capacity, the testing intensity per chip skyrockets. Teradyne becomes a leveraged play on that complexity. The stock's gain signals that investors are pricing in a prolonged capex cycle for AI chip testing, not just a one-time buildout.

What you need to know is that these moves validate a sector rotation into second and third derivative AI names. Nvidia's earnings momentum and guidance have given investors confidence to look beyond the obvious hyperscaler capex plays and into essential, less glamorous suppliers. Skyworks and Teradyne are not direct competitors—one enables connectivity, the other ensures manufacturing quality—but both share a common catalyst: Nvidia's insatiable appetite for advanced silicon is forcing the entire semiconductor food chain to expand.

For investors, the takeaway is clear. When Nvidia sneezes, the chip sector used to catch a cold. Today, when Nvidia accelerates, names like Skyworks and Teradyne catch a strong tailwind. The trade-up is a rational repricing of their AI exposure, often underrepresented in consensus models. Keep watching for similar moves in other overlooked players that supply into the AI compute stack; Nvidia's gravitational field is growing, and it is pulling many more orbits into its path.
