Tigress Financial has raised its 12-month price target on NVIDIA to $200, up from a previous $160, while reiterating a Strong Buy rating on the stock. The upgrade, which now implies an upside of more than 60% from current levels, arrives at a pivotal moment when global skepticism toward AI spending is rising and competitive pressure from Chinese models like DeepSeek is being dissected by the market. Yet this analyst action makes a clear statement: the conviction in NVIDIA's hardware moat remains unshakable.

The bullish call is anchored in data center momentum and the upcoming Blackwell platform, which Tigress views as a generational leap in accelerated computing. The firm's thesis points to AI capex from hyperscalers staying robust through 2025, with a broadening beyond cloud giants into sovereign AI and enterprise adoption. While some observers have questioned whether lower-cost inference models from China could erode demand for NVIDIA's premium GPUs, Tigress effectively dismisses that concern. The analyst note emphasizes that frontier model training, continuous retraining of large-scale systems, and the sheer scale of real-time inference workloads remain firmly tied to NVIDIA's CUDA ecosystem and hardware performance.

For the semiconductor and AI landscape, this price target lift serves as a counterweight to the narrative that DeepSeek-style efficiency breakthroughs might cap NVIDIA's growth. The maintenance of a Strong Buy rating signals that the core value proposition—full-stack dominance from silicon to software—is intact, and that any attempt to bypass NVIDIA's GPUs still confronts a deep moat built on years of developer lock-in, networking integration, and supply chain mastery. Tigress joins a chorus of analysts who see NVIDIA's valuation as justified by a multi-year capex cycle, not a speculative spike.

Ultimately, Tigress Financial's move is more than a numbers tweak. It is a reaffirmation that NVIDIA remains the primary beneficiary of the AI buildout, even as the competitive landscape becomes more crowded. For investors, the message is straightforward: the AI infrastructure trade is far from over, and NVIDIA continues to command a central role in the global semiconductor power race, irrespective of cheaper alternatives emerging from China.
